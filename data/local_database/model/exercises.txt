Ab Wheel    


FOCUS AREA Abs, Shoulders, Back
EQUIPMENT Wheel Roller

PREPARATION

Put the wheel roller right under your shoulders on the
floor. Kneel on the floor and hold the handles of the
wheel roller with your arms extended.

EXECUTION

1 Inhale and slowly roll the wheel forward until your
torso is parallel to the ground.

2. Pause for a few seconds when your body is fully
extended.

3 Exhale and pull the wheel back toward yourself to
the starting position and repeat.

KEY TIPS

1 To add difficulty, leave only your feet on the floor.

2. Keep a straight back and braced core during the
exercise.

Alternating Punch


FOCUS AREA Shoulders, Triceps, Chest
EQUIPMENT Bodyweight

PREPARATION

Stand with feet shoulder width apart. Keep a soft
bend in your knees. Bend your elbows and clench
your fists in front of your face.

EXECUTION
1 Extend one arm forward as fast as you can.
2. Take the arm back to the starting position.

3 Complete alternating between left and right.

KEY TIPS

1 Punch in line with your shoulder.

2. Keep a straight back and braced core during the
exercise.

3 Keep your wrists in line with your forearms.

Arnold Press: Dumbbell


FOCUS AREA Shoulders, Triceps
EQUIPMENT Dumbbell, Flat Bench

PREPARATION

Sit on a bench with feet on the floor. Hold a dumbbell
in each hand with elbows bent at around 45 degrees
at your sides and palms facing your body.


EXECUTION

1 Rotate your hands until your palms are facing
forward and squeeze your shoulders to press the
dumbbells vertically until your arms are straight
overhead.

2. Pause for a few seconds, slowly lower your arms
and rotate hands back to the starting position
and repeat.

KEY TIPS
1 Do not lock out your elbows at the top.

2 Do not rest dumbbells on your shoulders.

3 Keep a straight back and braced core during the
exercise.

Bench Jump


FOCUS AREA Glutes, Quadriceps, Calves
EQUIPMENT Bodyweight

PREPARATION

Place a bench in front of you and stand with feet
shoulder width apart. Bend a little at the knees.
Swing your arms straight behind you.

EXECUTION

1 Drive through heels, knees and hips to jump onto
the bench.

2. Swing your arms forward and bent,

3 Land on the bench with knees bent as softly as
possible.

4 Jump back down with knees bent to the starting
position and repeat.

KEY TIPS

1 To avoid joint injuries, keep your knees bent and
tiptoes be in the same direction with them.

2 Keep a straight back and braced core during the
exercise.

3 Ensure the bench is steady for security.

4 Lower the height of the bench if you land in a
deep squat rather than a quarter squat.

EQUIPMENT Handle Band

PREPARATION

Stand on the band with feet shoulder width apart.
Put the band under your midfeet and hold a handle in
each hand in front of your thighs in an overhand grip.

EXECUTION

1 Exhale and lift the handles straight forward to
the height of your head.

2. Pause for a few seconds, inhale and return to the
starting position slowly and repeat.

KEY TIPS
1 Keep your arms straight and elbows slightly bent
during the exercise.

2. Change the way you grip to emphasize different
muscle trainings.

3 Keep a straight back and braced core during the
exercise.

Front Squat: Barbell

FOCUS AREA Quadriceps, Glutes
EQUIPMENT Barbell

PREPARATION

Stand with feet shoulder width apart. Position the 
barbell across your front deltoids and hold it with
your arms crossed horizontally.


EXECUTION

1 Inhale and squat down until your thighs are
parallel to the floor.

2. Hold for a few seconds to feel the contraction.

3 Exhale, drive through the heels and midfoot to
the starting position slowly and repeat.

KEY TIPS
1 Keep the tiptoes in the same direction as your
knees.
2 Do not let your knees go over your toes.
3 Do not lock out your knees at the top.
4 Keep your glutes, quadriceps and hamstrings engaged

Hang Clean: Barbell

FOCUS AREA Full Body
EQUIPMENT Barbell

PREPARATION

Stand with feet shoulder width apart. Hold the bar
with ands slightly wider than shoulder width apart
in an overhand grip and arms extended.

EXECUTION

1 Drive your hips up and straighten your legs to pull
the barbell up.

2 Once the barbell passes your mid-thigh, jump.
slightly and drop your body under the bar with
your elbows facing forward and palms facing up.

3 As you receive the barbell, lower your hips to a
front squat position.

4 Brace you core and drive through your feet to
stand up straight.

5 Lower the barbell to the starting position slowly
and repeat.

KEY TIPS

1 Do not let your knees go over your toes when you
squat.
2 To add difficulty, add weights gradually.

3 Keep your knees and elbows slightly bent during
the exercise.

FOCUS AREA Abs
EQUIPMENT Bodyweight

PREPARATION

Lie on the floor with arms stretched to your sides to
stabilize your body. Lift your legs straight together
off the ground until they are vertical to the floor.

EXECUTION

1 Twist your legs to one side while keeping your
upper body in place and your knees and feet
together.

2. Pause for a few seconds and return to the
starting position slowly.

3 Complete alternating between left and right.

KEY TIPS

1 Do not let your legs bounce on the floor fo assist
the movement.

2. Keep your legs straight and together throughout
the movement.

3 To add difficulty, keep your legs straight.

4 Keep a braced core during the exercise.

Wrist Curl: Band
 

FOCUS AREA Forearms
EQUIPMENT Handle Band, Flat Bench

PREPARATION

Sit down and secure the band under one foot. Rest
one forearm on your thigh with the wrist ahead of
the knee. Hold the handle with an underhand grip.

EXECUTION
1 Exhale and curl your working wrist up.
2 Pause for a few seconds at the top position.
3 Inhale and return to the starting position and
repeat.

KEY TIPS
1 Use the opposite hand to secure the exercised
forearm.

2. Keep your thighs and the exercised forearm
parallel to the floor,

3 Keep your working elbow and wrist at the same
height during the exercise.

Wrist Roller: Plate
 

FOCUS AREA Forearms
EQUIPMENT Weight Plate

PREPARATION

Stand with feet shoulder width apart. Hold the
wrist roller with hands shoulder width apart in an
overhand grip and arms horizontal.

EXECUTION

1 Rotate your wrists alternatively onward to
bring the plate up to the bar by rolling the rope
around the roller.

2. Lower the plate to the starting position slowly by
rotating your wrists alternatively outward once it
reaches the bar.

KEY TIPS

1 Start with the rope unwrapped around the roller.

2. Keep your torso upright and move your forearms
and fists only.

3 Keep your elbows and wrists at the same height
during the exercise.
